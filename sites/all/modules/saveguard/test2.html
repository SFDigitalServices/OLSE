There are rules and regulations you must follow in order to legally rent out your residence. First, determine whether you are eligible to rent out your residence. If you meet the eligibility requirements, you must then consider details such as how long you would like to host guests and whether you will be on the premise when you have guests. These decisions affect how much you can earn and how many days per year you can rent out your residential unit. Anyone in San Francisco who earns rent from a short-term residential rental is considered a business and may owe taxes. You must obtain a Business Registration Certificate and pay applicable taxes (through the Treasurer and Tax Collector’s Office) and register with the Office of Short-Term Rentals.

 

 

If you are a new applicant...

Begin by going through the Short-Term Residential Rental Starter Kit. The kit contains everything you need to get started: a step-by-step guide, related documents, and permits necessary to be compliant.

 

If you already have a Business Registration Certificate and a Business Account Number... 

Update your account information online and declare use of a Qualified Website Company (Airbnb) and/or your status as a “Small Operator”.  All hosts with a Business Registration Certificate must update their account information in order to get the correct tax filing information and instructions.

 

Follow the instructions on this document. You will be able to declare your use of a Qualified Website Company and qualify for “Small Operator” designation to file and pay TOT annually, instead of monthly.

- Treasurer and Tax Collector Online New Business Registration for Qualified Hosts

(All hosts must obtain Business Registration Certificate and sign up for appropriate tax filing and payment schedule.)

 

- Treasurer and Tax Collector Online Declaration for Qualified Website Company and Small Operator Status  

(All hosts with a Business Registration Certificate must update their account information in order to get the correct tax filing information and instructions.)

 

- Treasurer and Tax Collector Certificate of Authority (COA)

(All hosts need a COA unless you only use a Qualified Website Company for your listings, or you earn $40,000 or less in rent per year and request to be designated as a Small Operator. Details)
